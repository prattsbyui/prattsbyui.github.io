Design Principles

Proximity

Amazon

amazon.com

This drop-menu from amazon is a great example of proximity. On smart phones it’s important the user taps the intended link or button with their finger. If links are too close or too small it can lead to a frustrating user experience. Thankfully, the links in this drop-menu have plenty of spacing to accommodate even the fattest of digits.

Alignment

ESPN

espn.com

This is a very common alignment. Go to almost any store on the web and you’ll find the site logo in the top-left and account options on the top-right. ESPN does the same thing here. You also notice the alignment of other objects on this like different sport’s logos and the articles. It’s a fast way to tell the user what items are related or not related.

Repitition

Wikipedia

wikipedia.org

Wikipedia’s use of repetition is evident in their use of color. Ever link on a wiki page is blue. We see repetition of alignment where we see logo, title, and description. We also see it down in the language options. Every language gets a little dot next to it.
