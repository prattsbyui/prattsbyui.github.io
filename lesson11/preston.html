What Conditions Are Good For Roofing?

By Sean Pratt

So, you are planning to work on your roof. Maybe its leaking, maybe you want a new look, or maybe put up holiday lights. Whatever reason you decide to work on the roof one thing to always consider is the weather. thank goodness for Rain/Shine, am I right? The obvious answer is to work on your roof in clear weather and ideal temperatures; however, other conditions are also safe for working on your roof. They require more preparation and planning. When it comes to roofing, if it’s raining, you’re out of luck. Not a good idea to tear apart you’re roof during a deluge. Thankfully temperature is not much of a concern except for comfort. If it’s hot try to keep cool. If it’s cold try to keep warm. In cold conditions be sure to watch for ice. If you have to go on your roof in snowy of icy condition because you are putting up Christmas decorations we recommend working from your ladder as much as possible. Cleated shoes would also be a good investment. Make sure they are metal as plastic cleats to slip easily.

We hope this article was helpful to you. It’s our hope that you use our site to keep yourself inform so you can make the best choices possible while working in any sort of weather.
